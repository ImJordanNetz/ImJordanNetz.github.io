SLEEP

Sleep is a crucially underrated aspect of our health and happiness that has a bigger effect on our daily activities than you might think. Sleep is essential for effective studying because it allows the brain to consolidate and retain the information learned during each day. During sleep, the brain engages in a process called memory consolidation, where it organizes and strengthens the neural connections formed during studying. This process is crucial for encoding new information into long-term memory and ensuring that it can be retrieved and applied later on. Additionally, sleep is essential for cognitive function, attention, and decision-making, all of which are crucial for useful studying. Without enough sleep, students may struggle with concentration, memory recall, and creative problem-solving, hindering their ability to perform well at school.

How much sleep do you need?

Hours: Effects:
1 Hour Not enough sleep to really count - will increase tiredness and physical function
2 Hours Very sleep deprived, major risk of accidents
3 Hours Impaired motor function and decreased accuracy
4 Hours Indadequate sleep - irritability and decreased motivation
5 Hours Reduced creativity and increased risk of obesity
6 Hours Somewhat inadequate sleep, decreased productivity
7 Hours Reccomended amount of sleep for most adults
8 Hours Provides the best amount of sleep for cognitive and physical function
9 Hours May be beneficial for some people, not much of a real benefit
10 Hours Excessive sleep - leads to grogginess and impaired cognitive function
